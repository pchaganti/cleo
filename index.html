    How I Title My Blog Posts
    Yesterday, I read a post by Bearly Significant titled: "Headlines Terrify Me". The post talked about the nuances of creating the perfect headline, that one time they made a typo, and their fear of sabotaging themselves with their words. The post starts: Crafting an effective headline is an art form mastered only by the finest journalists and editors. In fewer words than a tweet, all nuances and "yes, buts" must be simplified. This is crucial because journalists are taught to always consider: "if someone only reads the headline, will they be somewhat informed?" Although the blog post was geared more towards the news sector, I can’t help but think that there is some parallelism with how bloggers craft their blog post titles. I think everyone has their own flavor of titling their posts that works with their niche, and blogging voice. I follow a few who title their blog posts… In a super vague way that makes you want to click on the post to understand what their title means Short and straight to the point - you know exactly what you’re getting yourself into and the topic of the post A poetic title that encompasses the topic of the post A few words excerpt from their actual post …and so on. I think I do a mixture of some of the above. I tend to lean towards a blog title that evokes the same emotion as what I’m writing about. If it’s a more poetic piece, I title it as such. If it’s more casual, the title also tends to be. Although there are times when my posts tend to be vague, the goal is to rarely make the title vague. I always want you to know what you’re getting into so you can choose to read, or pass on my posts. I hate clickbait, and so I try to avoid that as much as I can. My blog is pretty straight forward.
